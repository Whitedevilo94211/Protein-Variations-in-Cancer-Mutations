Protein Variations in Cancer Mutations

Proteins are the functional building blocks of life, and their structures and functions are tightly regulated by genetic sequences. However, mutations in key genes can lead to significant protein variations, which are often implicated in cancer development and progression. These variations can arise from single nucleotide changes, insertions, deletions, or structural rearrangements that alter the amino acid sequence of a protein Identifying and understanding these protein variations is critical for cancer research, biomarker discovery, and targeted therapy development. Resources such as COSMIC, ClinVar, and UniProt provide extensive databases of cancer-related protein mutations, helping researchers analyze their structural and functional impact. Structural modeling and computational predictions further aid in assessing how these variations affect protein stability, interactions, and drug sensitivity.

This database provides critical information on protein structural variations in cancer mutations, including key genes and proteins involved,post-translational modifications (PTMs), cellular localization of proteins, and 3D structures.
